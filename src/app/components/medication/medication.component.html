Medication Information

Metformin Diabetes Medication

Metformin is a medicine used to treat type 2 diabetes, and to help prevent type 2 diabetes if you're at high risk of developing it.

Metformin works by reducing the amount of sugar your liver releases into your blood. It also makes your body respond better to insulin. Insulin is the hormone that controls the level of sugar in your blood.

It's best to take metformin with a meal to reduce the side effects.

The most common side effects are feeling and being sick, diarrhoea, stomach ache and going off your food.
Jardiance Diabetes Medication

Jardiance is a medicine used to treat /type 2 diabetes,

Jardiance works by helping the kidneys remove glucose from the bloodstream through the urine

By removing excessive glucose from the body through urine, Jardiance helps to improve glycemic control among patients with type 2 diabetes. This can result in improved HbA1c levels, which can reduce the risk of diabetes-related complications.

The most common mild side effects are urinating more often than usual, dehydration, nausea and increased thirst.
Gliclazide Diabetes Medication

Gliclazide is a medicine used to treat type 2 diabetes.

Gliclazide lowers your blood sugar by increasing the amount of insulin your body produces.

Tablets should be taken twice daily and according to the main meals of the day.

Gliclazide can sometimes make your blood sugar level too low (hypoglycaemia). Carry some sweets or fruit juice with you to help when this happens.

The most common side effects are abdominal pain; diarrhoea; hypoglycaemia; nausea.
Simvastatin Statin Medication

Simvastatin belongs to a group of medicines called statins.

It's used to lower cholesterol if you've been diagnosed with high blood cholesterol. It's also taken to prevent heart disease, including heart attacks and strokes.

Used in conjunction with people taking medication for diabetes like Metformin.

It's unusual to have any side effects but don't take it with Grapefruit juice.
